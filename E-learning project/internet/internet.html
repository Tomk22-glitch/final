1. Definition & Evolution

The Internet is a global network of interconnected computer systems that enables the transfer of data, information, and multimedia. It uses communication protocols like TCP/IP and HTTPS to securely exchange information worldwide.

  • Origins: The Internet began as ARPANET in 1969, designed for research and military communication. It evolved from dial-up connections and packet-switching methods to modern broadband, fiber optics, and 5G networks.

2. Modern Connectivity

  • Broadband & Fiber: Provide fast, always-on connections ideal for streaming and large data transfers.
  • Wi-Fi: Wireless local area networks for homes, schools, and offices.
  • Mobile Networks: 4G and 5G technologies deliver high-speed access on mobile devices.
  • Key Hardware: Modems and routers manage wired and wireless data transfer. Data moves through cables or via radio waves for Wi-Fi and cellular networks.

3. Internet Services

World Wide Web (WWW)

  • Browsers: Modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari) include privacy modes, security tools, and extensions.
  • Websites & Apps: Hosted on servers, accessed via URLs. E-commerce, social media, and e-learning sites are common examples.

Email

  • Basics: Uses SMTP for sending, POP3 or IMAP for receiving. Web-based services like Gmail and Outlook offer multimedia support and calendar integration.
  • Structure: Username, “@” symbol, and domain (e.g., yourname@gmail.com).
  • Features: Spam filtering, phishing protection, two-factor authentication (2FA), and cloud integration are standard.

4. Cybersecurity & Safe Use

  • Strong Passwords & 2FA: Use unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Secure Connections: Check for “https://” and the padlock icon when browsing sensitive sites.
  • Regular Updates: Keep software updated to prevent vulnerabilities.
  • Awareness: Recognize phishing, malware, and suspicious links. Don’t open unknown attachments.
  • Privacy: Control what you share on social media. Use privacy settings and back up data securely on reputable cloud services.

5. Practical Tips

Web Browsing

  • Bookmark favorite sites for easy access.
  • Use navigation tools: Back, Forward, Refresh, and Home buttons.
  • Search smartly with precise keywords and operators.

Email Etiquette

  • Use clear subject lines and polite, concise language.
  • Proofread before sending. Attach only relevant, virus-free files.
  • Organize with folders and labels for better management.

Productivity

  • Integrate calendars, tasks, and cloud services for efficiency.
  • Use filters and rules to sort emails automatically.
